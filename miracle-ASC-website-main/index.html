ASC - All-Sky Cameras

All-Sky cameras are used for imaging auroral borealis with special optical elements to get an image of the whole sky in one shot

Imaging the auroras

All-sky cameras (ASC) that are used for imaging auroral borealis have special optical elements such as fish-eye lenses or spherical mirrors to acquire an image of the whole sky in one shot (hence the name). One ASC image covers a circular area with a diameter of about 600 km at 110 km altitude.

Finnish Meteorological Institute (FMI) operates several all-sky cameras in wintertime with the objective to collect long-term auroral observations in Finland. In research auroral camera data are often combined with other space- or ground-based observations, e.g. with magnetometer data by the IMAGE network or with ionospheric radar data.

These pages present technical information and quick-look data of FMI auroral cameras covering all generations from the old film cameras to the newest color and EMCCD cameras.
